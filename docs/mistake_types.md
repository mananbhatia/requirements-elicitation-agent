Follow-up questions:
• Fail to elicit tacit assumptions, i.e., fail to justify or authorize assumptions stakeholders tacitly make without justification. 
• Fail to consider alternatives, i.e., fail to look for alternative information or alternatives to existing requirements.
• No clarification when unclear, i.e., accepts what interviewee said without asking for clarification when words interviewee said are unclear.
• No clarification when contradictory, i.e., accepts what interviewee said without asking for clarification when words interviewee said are contradictory. 
• Fail to elicit tacit knowledge, i.e., fail to elicit tacit knowledge known to interviewee but unknown to interviewer. 
Question framing:
• Ask a generic, domain-independent question, i.e., fail to ask a question related to the interview domain or the question asked is too generic.
• Ask a question that is too long or articulated, i.e., ask a question too long or complicated that would likely require interviewee to ask for repeating or rephrasing multiple times.
• Use jargon, i.e., ask a question containing special words or expressions not in the common vocabulary and is difficult for interviewees to understand. 
• Ask a technical question, i.e., ask a question that requires technical knowledge in order to answer. 
• Ask a question inappropriate to user’s profile, i.e., ask a question that cannot be answered by the interviewee given the interviewee’s profile.
• Ask for solutions, i.e., ask interviewee to present a solution to satisfy a requirement.
• Ask a question that involves multiple kinds of requirements, i.e., mix different categories of requirements or multiple specific requirements within one category into a single question.
• Ask a vague question that leads to multiple interpretations, i.e., ask a question that can be interpreted in more than one way. 
• Ask a vague question which could infer no reasonable meaning, i.e., ask a question that does not have enough context or clarity for interviewee to answer.
